Fig. 01 — About Me

Painting what's within reach

Ana Duarte trained as a printmaker before turning to oil and gouache, and it still shows: her canvases are built up in flat, deliberate layers, the way a plate is inked before a press run. She paints what's close by — fruit split open on the studio table, laundry on the line, the particular green of a fig leaf in late August.

The work stays deliberately unfinished at the edges. Raw linen shows through the paint, pencil lines are left visible, and drips are rarely wiped away. It's a way of keeping the labour of looking in the final piece, not just the conclusion of it.

Ana lives and works outside Faro, Portugal, in a studio converted from her grandmother's fig-drying shed — the source of the name, and of most of the fruit in her still lifes.

A working record

    Group show, Terreno Comum

    Galeria Almargem, Faro. Six painters working from the Algarve landscape and domestic interior.

  2. 2024

    Printmaking residency

    Casa das Artes, Porto. Six weeks developing the monotype series that became Orchard Studies.

  3. 2023

    First solo presentation, Still Warm

    Studio open days, Faro. Twelve still-life paintings shown in the working studio, unframed.

  4. 2021

    BFA, Painting & Printmaking

    Faculdade de Belas Artes, University of Lisbon. Graduated with distinction.
